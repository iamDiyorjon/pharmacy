📄 Business Model Document
Project: Telegram Pharmacy Pickup Platform
1. Vision

Create a Telegram-based pharmacy ordering system that:

Allows users to search medicines

Upload prescriptions

Receive calculated offers

Pick up prepared medicines without waiting

Long-term goal:
Transform from a single-owner pharmacy tool into a multi-pharmacy marketplace platform.

2. Problem Statement

Customers face these problems:

They don’t know which pharmacy has the required medicine.

They must wait in line.

They don’t know the total price before arriving.

Prescription processing takes time inside the pharmacy.

Pharmacies face these problems:

Manual queue pressure.

No digital ordering channel.

No structured customer database.

No online positioning.

3. Solution Overview

A Telegram Mini App that connects customers and pharmacies.

Phase 1: Pickup-based ordering.
Phase 2: Delivery integration.
Phase 3: Multi-pharmacy marketplace.

4. Business Model Phases
🟢 Phase 1 — Own Pharmacies (Validation Phase)
Objective:

Validate demand and optimize internal workflow.

Model:

Only owner’s pharmacies are listed.

Orders are prepared in advance.

Pickup only.

Customer Flow:

User searches medicine or uploads prescription.

Pharmacy manually calculates total.

User confirms.

Order prepared.

User picks up without waiting.

Revenue:

Direct pharmacy sales.

No commission model yet.

Key Goal:

Reach consistent weekly order volume and validate user behavior.

🟡 Phase 2 — Operational Optimization
Objective:

Increase efficiency and improve user experience.

Improvements:

Faster processing

Structured order management

Basic analytics

Customer return rate tracking

Still pickup-first model.

Revenue remains direct sales.

🔵 Phase 3 — Multi-Pharmacy Platform
4
Objective:

Transform into marketplace.

Model:

Multiple pharmacies onboarded.

Each pharmacy manages its own orders.

Platform controls user interface and traffic.

Revenue Options:

Commission per order (3–7%)

Monthly SaaS subscription

Priority listing fee

Delivery revenue share

5. Value Proposition
For Customers:

No waiting

Transparent pricing

Medicine availability visibility

Faster service

Convenience via Telegram

For Pharmacies:

Additional sales channel

Queue reduction

Digital presence

Customer retention

Structured order management

6. Competitive Advantage

Unique strengths:

Owner controls supply (2 pharmacies already).

Immediate testing environment.

Low initial risk.

Telegram ecosystem (high local penetration).

Pickup-first reduces logistics complexity.

7. Go-To-Market Strategy

Stage 1:

Launch quietly.

Promote inside own pharmacies.

QR code at cashier.

Staff encourage usage.

Stage 2:

Target repeat customers.

Introduce small incentives (priority service).

Stage 3:

Approach other pharmacies with real data:

Order volume

Customer retention

Revenue increase

8. Cost Structure

Early stage:

Development time

Hosting

Staff training

Later stage:

Support team

Marketing

Delivery partnerships

Sales team for pharmacy onboarding

9. Key Metrics (Non-Technical)

Daily active users

Orders per day

Average order value

Order confirmation rate

Repeat customer rate

Pharmacy processing time

10. Risk Analysis

Regulatory:

Prescription medicine handling compliance.

Operational:

Staff resistance to new workflow.

Inventory mismatch.

Incorrect manual calculations.

Market:

Low adoption rate.

Customers prefer traditional buying.

Strategic:

Large pharmacy chains launching similar solution.

11. Expansion Strategy

Step 1: Validate with 2 pharmacies.
Step 2: Stabilize operations.
Step 3: Add 3–5 external pharmacies.
Step 4: Introduce commission model.
Step 5: Launch delivery.

12. Long-Term Vision

Become:

A pharmacy marketplace.

A pharmacy SaaS system.

Or a hybrid platform with logistics.

Potential exit options:

Acquisition by pharmacy chain.

Acquisition by delivery platform.

Merge with health-tech ecosystem.

13. Core Strategic Decision

Short-Term Focus:
Operational optimization and validation.

Long-Term Focus:
Platform business.
